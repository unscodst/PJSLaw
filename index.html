Information on Trials

  • DUI/OVI - Criminal Trial

    DUI/OVI

    Alcohol- related traffic charges, such as driving under the influence (DUI) and operating a vehicle while under the influence (OVI) can result in serious and lifelong consequences. It is important to consult with an experienced OVI/DUI defense attorney in order to learn your legal options and protect your legal rights. Patty Smith, as a member of NCDD, OACDL, and DUIDLA, has attended hundreds of hours of training specifically for the defense of DUI/OVI.

    Ohio DUI Penalties

    Ohio has some of the strictest penalties for DUI/OVI in the country. Penalties for DUI/OVI can be severe, even for a first offense. If convicted, you could face license suspension, hefty fines, vehicle immobilization, required OVI offender license plates, ignition interlock, and mandatory jail time. If you test over the legal limit or fail to submit to a breath alcohol test, you face a mandatory minimum Administrative License Suspension (ALS) unless you correctly appeal the suspension.

    Aside from court-mandated penalties, your insurance premiums will likely increase and drunk driving charges can do significant harm to your reputation There are hundreds of collateral consequences aside from already mentioned legal consequences. Court-imposed driving limitations may also impact your ability to get to and from work.

    Patricia Smith handles all matters related to the defense of your drunk driving charge, including challenging the reliability of breathalyzer equipment used, appearing in criminal court as your advocate, and negotiating with the prosecution to obtain a reduced sentence. As a skilled DUI attorney, Patricia Smith understands the Ohio drunk driving laws.

  • Felony - Criminal Trial

    Felonies

    Felony criminal charges are very serious matters. They can have long-lasting consequences. Felony convictions can result in prison, ranging from 6 months to life, as well as substantial fines. Felony charges can cause significant damage to your reputation even if you are not convicted. A felony conviction can affect your employment, ability to obtain employment, right to vote, and right to possess a firearm.

    As an experienced criminal attorney, Patricia Smith works hand in hand with highly knowledgeable experts to assist in the defense of your case.
